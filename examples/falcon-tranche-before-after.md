# Example: Falcon tranche planning

## Prompt
Should we proceed with pilot if readiness is incomplete?

## Typical AI answer
> It depends on your risk tolerance. Sometimes moving forward helps uncover issues earlier.

## Structured Thinking Partner answer
> Readiness is incomplete. Proceeding now is likely to shift operational burden onto support functions and users, increase rework, and weaken failure containment.
>
> What is known:
> - critical readiness evidence is incomplete
> - support and data flows are not yet demonstrably coherent end-to-end
>
> What is missing:
> - confirmation of operational support readiness
> - assurance on key dependency flows
> - credible containment boundary if issues emerge
>
> What the decision depends on:
> - whether failure is explicitly acceptable
> - whether it can be contained within pilot scope
>
> Recommendation:
> Hold unless leadership explicitly accepts the consequences and the containment boundary is credible.
